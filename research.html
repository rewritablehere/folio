Research

Conceptual framework for AI Assistants to navigate contexts in conversations

Abstract

Spaces are defined in multiple ways and handled uniquely in a conversation (needs referencing). It is as important as the subject itself many-a-times (needs referencing). A look at what defines spatial contexts for human beings may help us present it as a boundaries as well as foundations for an AI Assistant to be spatially aware. By giving it a knowhow of 'whats-what' about a space, it could be that much more accurate in percieving a context in a conversation with a human being.

Background

Suppose a conceptual landscape can be created for every conversation. Subject(s) and object(s) become physical points in this landscape. The physical environment of where the conversation is happenning translates to the main attribute containing or bounding this landscape. Let us try and demarkate quadrants in it. Suppose we have one side representing the beginning state of a conversation. The end state could be associated to the opposite quadrant. In many cases the conversation will probably not have a beginning and an end state(s) but rather they coincide in the landscape. But many conversations will contain an unique starting point and an end point in the landscape that best defines the conversation. It could very well be connected landscape areas as there could be multiple contexts here as well but for arriving at something concrete initially we can look at simple conversations Could the inflection of a string of words in the conversation point to or can be translated to a journey of subject(s) and object(s) across this landscape? Consider defining the conversation graphically as system of spaces and vectors. There could be bounds defined in the conversation from what's derivable as subject(s) and objects(s) and their current states. A vector has both the magnitude as well as direction. This could be used to identify what tilts it and towards which quadrant. For simplicity all of this can be attempted in a 2 dimensional space.

Research Question

Can we arrive at objective definitions of contexts? Can this be possible by collecting a vast data set relevant to a single dimension of a person's context? Can we look at finding invariants in that dataset? Can we establish relationships from studying about each point and its variance(Ïƒ2)?
