Research

Conceptual framework for AI Assistants to navigate contexts in conversations

#ComputerVision #SceneUnderstanding #ArtificialGeneralIntelligence

Abstract

Artificial intelligence enabled personal digital assistants can now have sophisticated conversations with human beings and respond in context.[1] NLP has enabled conversations to be weighed, processed and plotted (needs referencing). They do so by having the entire training dataset pass into the the machine learning algorithm. This corpus enables AI assistant to predict the result required. What defines contexts for human beings may be presented as boundaries as well as foundations for grounding an AI Assistant in it. By giving an AI Assistant a working knowledge of each figment of a conversation's context, it could be that much more accurate in percieving a context in a conversation with a human being.

This working knowledge can be provided as a graphical user interface for the AI Assistant to navigate to a place in the interface where it relates to each context element

Background

At the most skeletal level, Machine learning involves split data into groups. Deep reinforcement learning procedures demand large amounts of training data [1]. For solution to any machine learning problem, we identify the error and go about minimizing the error. I was looking at a different approach for this step. We have a problem in front of us. Suppose a conceptual landscape can be created for every conversation. Subject(s) and object(s) and every construct or element in the conversation become physical points in this landscape. Additionally everything that can inflect into the meaning of the conversation, including say, the physical environment of where the conversation is happenning translates an attribute the landscape. Let us try and demarkate quadrants. Suppose we have one side representing the beginning state of a conversation. The end state could be associated to the opposite quadrant. In many cases the conversation will probably not have a beginning and an end state(s) but rather they coincide in the landscape. But many conversations will contain an unique starting point and an end point in the landscape that best defines the conversation. It could very well be many connected landscape areas as there could be multiple contexts but for arriving at something concrete initially we can look at simple conversation where landcape in a single overall entity. Could the inflection of a string of words in the conversation point to a direction in the landscape described earlier? Can the inflection of the words or the sentence be translated to a journey of subject(s) and object(s) across this landscape? Consider defining the conversation graphically as system of spaces and vectors. There could be bounds defined in the conversation from what's derivable as subject(s) and objects(s) and their current states. A vector has both the magnitude as well as direction. This could be used to identify what tilts it and towards which quadrant. For simplicity all of this can be attempted in a 2 dimensional space.

Research Question

Can we arrive at objective definitions of contexts? Can this be possible by collecting a vast data set relevant to a single dimension of a person's context? Can we look at finding invariants in that dataset? Can we establish relationships from studying about each point and its variance(Ïƒ2)?
